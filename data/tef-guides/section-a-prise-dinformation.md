# Section A – Prise d'information (Getting Information)

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Number of Questions** | A single question |
| **Duration** | Approximately 5 minutes |
| **Format** | A classified ad related to daily life (job offer, services, leisure, housing, etc.) |
| **Goal** | Get information about the document by asking questions |

## Official Instructions

**French:**
> « Vous avez vu cette petite annonce pour __________ et vous êtes intéressé(e). Vous téléphonez pour avoir plus d'informations. Posez une dizaine de questions. Votre examinateur(trice) joue le rôle de votre interlocuteur(trice). »

**English:**
> You saw this classified ad for __________ and you are very interested. You give them a phone call in order to gather more information about the ad. Ask about ten questions. The examiner will be your conversation partner for this exercise.

## Strategies for Success

### Tip #1 – Practice Formulating and Asking Questions

**Key Rule: Subject-Verb Inversion**

In French, when you ask a question, you must put the verb **BEFORE** the subject. Additionally, if the verb ends and the subject begins with a vowel or a silent letter, you need to add a **"t"** between the verb and subject.

**Examples:**
- Il habite à Paris. → **Habite-t-il à Paris ?**
- Elle va à la plage. → **Va-t-elle à la plage ?**

### Main Interrogative Adverbs

#### **"Quoi / Que"**: What
Informs us about the nature of something.

**Example:**
- Ex: Que vendez-vous dans ce magasin ?
- EN: What do you sell in this shop?

#### **"Qui"**: Who
Informs us about the identity of the person who is doing the action.

**Example:**
- Ex: Qui est le directeur du magasin ?
- EN: Who is the shop's manager?

#### **"Quand"**: When
Gives us the date, the moment of the action.

**Example:**
- Ex: Quand avez-vous ouvert ?
- EN: When did you open?

#### **"Où"**: Where
Gives us the place of the action.

**Example:**
- Ex: Où la boutique est-elle située ?
- EN: Where is the shop located?

#### **"Comment"**: How
Informs us about the conditions, the manner in which the action is performed.

**Example:**
- Ex: Comment votre magasin s'appelle-t-il ?
- EN: How is your shop called?

### Alternative Question Structure: "Est-ce que"

You can also ask a question by using **"est-ce que"** at the beginning of a sentence. In this case, you don't need to reverse the position of the subject and the verb.

**Examples:**
- Elle va à la plage. → **Est-ce qu'elle va à la plage ?**
- Que vendez-vous dans ce magasin ? → **Qu'est-ce que vous vendez dans ce magasin ?**

### Tip #2 – Avoid Generic Questions, Ask Relevant Questions

The examiners usually notice when candidates recite questions they have learnt by heart and penalize them for it. Try to find questions that are relevant to the classified ad. Better use simpler sentences reflecting your own level than questions learnt by heart that will ultimately disadvantage you.

### Tip #3 – Try to Have a Conversation with the Examiner

We advise you to try to develop an idea and create a meaningful exchange with the examiner rather than asking ten short questions in a row. Ideally, you must be able to react to the answers given to you by the examiner. This will:

- Prove that you understand what you are told
- Show that you are able to quickly formulate questions
- Demonstrate that your vocabulary is wide enough for you to adapt to the flow of the conversation

**Important Note:** This test does not evaluate your creativity, but your linguistic skills. The relevance of your questions is not taken into account in the assessment of this part of the test. You just need to ask questions that are about the ad. The most important is to speak in a fluent manner with a varied vocabulary.
