# Section B – Description d'une annonce et argumentation (Describing an Ad and Arguing)

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Number of Questions** | A single question |
| **Duration** | Approximately 10 minutes |
| **Format** | An advertisement or classified ad related to daily life (job offer, offer of services, leisure, public announcement, etc.) |
| **Goal** | Present the document to a friend and argue to convince them to participate in the situation you describe |

## Official Instructions

**French:**
> « Vous avez lu cette annonce. Vous en parlez à un(e) ami(e) qui __________.
> 
> Présentez-lui ce document.
> Essayez de le (la) convaincre de __________.
> Votre examinateur(trice) joue le rôle de votre interlocuteur(trice). »

**English:**
> You have read this ad. You are talking about it to a friend who __________.
> 
> Tell them what the document is about.
> Try to convince them to __________.
> The examiner will be your conversation partner for this exercise.

## Strategies for Success

### Tip #1 – Practice Presenting Your Arguments

Reviewing the vocabulary of argumentation can help you greatly for this exercise. You can start with the verbs used to express a piece of advice (je te conseille de, je te recommande de, etc.) as well as the linking words that will help you explain and justify your arguments.

**Key Vocabulary for Argumentation:**
- **Verbs of advice:** je te conseille de, je te recommande de, je te propose de
- **Linking words:** parce que, car, donc, c'est pourquoi, en effet, d'ailleurs, de plus, etc.

### Tip #2 – Use the Document and the Instructions to Your Advantage

The document and the instructions provide you with many clues to build up your argumentation. For example, the instructions can specify that your conversation partner likes to read and that you have to convince them to join a book club. You must take the information you are provided with into account to show the examiner that you have understood the instructions.

However, be careful not to repeat exactly what is written. You have to rephrase the indications of the document with your own words. **This is not a reading test!**

Key points to remember:
- Extract relevant information from the document
- Use the specific context given in the instructions (e.g., your friend's interests, preferences, or situation)
- Build arguments that directly address why your friend should participate
- Demonstrate comprehension by rephrasing, not reciting

## Section Characteristics

**Difference from Section A:**
- Longer duration (10 minutes vs. 5 minutes)
- More emphasis on persuasion and argumentation
- Requires structured presentation of ideas
- Calls for personal conviction and justification of claims
- Tests your ability to adapt language to a social context (talking to a friend)

**Key Skills Evaluated:**
- Clear presentation of information
- Persuasive communication
- Use of reasoning and justification
- Fluency and naturalness in conversation
- Appropriate use of vocabulary and sentence structures
